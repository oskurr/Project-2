Heatwave Deaths Are Rampant in Black and Brown Neighborhoods

Oscar Flores
November 17, 2024

NEW YORK - Climate change has been a topic of discussion among experts due to the high temperatures and hot summers. This November marked the hottest election day since 1936. Last summer, the city recorded 350 deaths due to high temperatures and heat strokes. However, the people suffering most are people of color in lower-class neighborhoods.

The city has listed Crown Heights, East Harlem, Mott Haven, Brownsville, and Coney Island as the top five neighborhoods with the highest count of victims. This is due to the lack of access to fair and affordable healthcare and lack of funding.
